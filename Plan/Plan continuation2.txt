1. Context and Standing Constraints
This plan continues SyntheticL2 from the state locked in as of Phase 130 (no-replay diagnostic baselines, 2026-07-19). It is written to stand on its own;
every claim below is supported by evidence already inside this repository.
Four standing constraints govern every phase and must not be relaxed:
1. Instrument scope is retail cash equity only. No futures, no options, no derivative-instrument synthetics. All strategies must be expressible as
marketable or passive orders on NSE cash equity in the current 32-symbol Phase 96 panel.
2. Depth ceiling is 5 levels. Zerodha WebSocket exposes Ll—L5 only. Phase 6 generator produces Ll—L5. No phase may use hidden-order models,
full order-book reconstruction, or exchange-side queue snapshots.
3. Costs are calibrated and are not the bottleneck. Phase 116 blocklisted all three Ll taker families ( DENSE ,
DENSE_se2 MICROPRICE, DENSE se3 IT MOMENTUM) as negative under every tested execution profile —
zero_latency_spread_only_control, retail marketable default , and stressed_retail . Signal weakness at Ll is the binding
constraint. This plan therefore places the burden of proof on the signal side (L2—L5 information content), not on execution cheapening.
o Pinned cost model: zerodha_equity_intraday_nse_order_formu1a_v2 2926 07 14 . Not editable within this plan.
o A harsh-stress variant of the cost model is added in Phase 131 so any surviving strategy is robust to plausible cost increases rather than
balanced on the base assumption.
4. No profitability or replay unlock inside this plan. strategy_replay_allowed = e remains the default. Only real-anchor gates (Phase
110/115/117) may unlock replay, and none are executed here. No phase may emit buy/sell signals, order arrival streams, fill models tagged as live,
or PnL replay tagged as promoted.

2. What The Repository Has Established
Distilled from Phases 41—130. This is the honest state of knowledge this plan builds on.
• Ll taker strategies do not clear retail cost on synthetic data. Roughly 800 million simulated trades across three families and three execution
profiles (Phase 52). Losses ranged from {1.5 B to {20 B annualized. Blocklisted in Phase 116.
• Same-family variants and rescue attempts also failed. Cross-symbol lead-lag (Phases 63, 77, 82, 84), cross-symbol regime imbalance (Phase 91),
low-turnover event windows (Phase 93), precommitted composite signals (Phase 87), passive queue capture (Phase 89), and lower-frequency meta
replays (Phases 60—61). None survived precommitted gates.
• Isolated "positive pockets" are not edges. Every failed rescue produced some months or symbols with positive PnL, but none produced an
accepted survivor under precommitted gates. This is now a standing rule: positive pockets do not rescue a full-sample negative verdict.
• The synthetic generator was rebuilt to be more realistic (Phases 79, 94—109) with symbol-aware calibration, anchor cadence and volatility
patches, and a residual-imbalance floor. Realism is asserted; it has not been adversarially audited.
• Real anchor L2 is not available. Phase 117 records 1 candidate day of unverified provenance and 4 more required for minimum unlock. The
drop-zone importer (Phases 113—115) is built but idle.

3. Diagnosis of the Remaining Surface
Two surfaces have never been genuinely tested on synthetic data:
• L2—L5 features used inside trading logic. The generator produces them; Phase 130 baselines occasionally read mean_15_depth as an input; but
no precommitted strategy family reads dynamic L2—L5 structure. Every falsified strategy so far consumed Ll only.
• Realistic passive/queue-capture execution. Phase 65 and Phase 89 probed this surface, but without a full queue-position x depth-level fill
model, per-level cancel intensity, or fat-tail latency jitter.
These are the only two axes where richer information could plausibly change the outcome for retail cash equity. Everything else at Ll is closed.
4. Objective
Exhaust the L2—L5 passive surface on synthetic data under realistic Zerodha retail cost stress, and end in one of three well-defined states:
• Falsified. Update the Phase 116 blocklist with a new deep-book passive entry and formally close synthetic strategy hunting.
• Marginal. Emit a machine-readable feature and strategy specification that can be re-evaluated the day real L2 data becomes available through the
Phase 113—115 drop-zone.
• Clean synthetic survivor. Same handoff artifact as marginal, plus an explicit advisory that a synthetic-only survivor is not deployable evidence and
must be re-tested on real L2 before any replay unlock is considered.
The plan is deliberately six phases, not sixty. Prior branch trees produced audit debt, not edge.

6. Forbidden Actions Inside This Plan
Reopening Ll -only taker strategy families. Phase 116 blocklist stands.
Vendor backfill of any historical L2 data.
Expanding the symbol universe beyond the current 32-symbol Phase 96 panel.
Changing the pinned Zerodha cost model or editing the harsh regime factors after Phase 131.
Flipping strategy _ replay _ allowed from any phase in this plan.
Emitting buy/sell signals, order arrival streams, live-tagged fill models, or promoted PnL replay.
Any RL, meta-learning, or ensemble on top of the Phase 134 strategies before Phase 136 verdict.
Adding new synthetic scenario diversity beyond Phase 791s audit while this plan is running.
7. Success Definition
Success is not a profitable synthetic strategy. Success is concluding the L2—L5 passive surface with one of the following clean outcomes:
1. Clean falsification (Phase 132 kill-switch, or Phase 135 verdict Outcome A) — synthetic deep-book passive research is formally closed and the
Phase 116 blocklist is updated. The correct next move is to wait on real anchor L2 acquisition rather than invent new synthetic branches.
2. Marginal handoff (Outcome B) — a precommit artifact exists that will be re-evaluated on real L2 the moment enough anchor days arrive.
3. Synthetic survivor with explicit advisory (Outcome C) — same handoff artifact plus a written statement that synthetic evidence alone is not
deployable.
If Phase 132 kill-switch fires, the plan terminates at Phase 132. That is a legitimate and preferred early-exit outcome. Both a clean falsification and a clean
handoff are equally valid successes; prolonging the branch to force a positive result is not.
